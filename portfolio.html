Data Science Project (completed with three other group members):
This project consists of an analysis of the demographics of US Police Shooting victims. Our initial question was: "Can certain demographics effect someone's chance of experiencing police violence?" After getting feedback from peers and cleaning our data, this question evolved to look at how many encounters actually became violent and how are levels of violence evolving based on age (police use of force for people over 50 vs. people under 30). New questions that were considered throughout the analysis: "How do the levels of force vary by race?" "How effective are certain types of force used?" API/Web-app Development Project (currently working on with three other group members):
COMING SOON! Imagine it's a Friday night and you're hanging out with friends and someone suggests that you all watch a movie. Sounds great, but how do you decide which movie to watch? GroupMovie makes this process easy. You can create a new virtual room and select which streaming services members of the group have. Once everyone has joined the room, voting begins. Different movies are recommended as the voting goes on and the preferences of the group are learned. Once the timer runs out, the top movies are put in a tournament and the group now has movies to watch that they actually want to see! No more wasting time trying to pick a movie that everyone will agree on, only to end up with nothing. Our app will make the process much faster and achieve results that everyone in the group is happy with.
